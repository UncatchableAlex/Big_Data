==Fictional character biography==
Walter Newell first appears in the title ''[[Tales to Astonish]]''  as an [[oceanography|oceanographer]] working for the [[United States]] government. The character encounters the human/[[Homo mermanus|Atlantean]] [[Hybrid (biology)|hybrid]] hero [[Namor|Namor the Sub-Mariner]] and his lover [[Lady Dorma]], while supervising the construction of a domed sub-sea city whose purpose is to harvest food for mankind. The city, however, is destroyed by the villain the [[Plunderer (character)|Plunderer]].<ref>''Tales to Astonish'' #95 (Sep. 1967). Marvel Comics.</ref> Newell becomes a perennial character in the title ''Prince Namor, the Sub-Mariner'' and aids the hero and Dorma against the villain [[Tiger Shark (Marvel Comics)|Tiger Shark]]<ref>''Prince Namor, the Sub-Mariner'' #16 (Aug. 1969). Marvel Comics.</ref> and is coerced by the government into investigating the disappearance of water from the Earth's oceans. This is attributed to [[Extraterrestrial life|aliens]] who are believed to be in league with Namor. Ordered to capture Namor, Newell develops a suit based on the form of the sea creature the [[manta ray]], and as 'Stingray' captures Namor, who was weakened at the time. Newell, however, believes Namor to be innocent and allows him to escape.<ref>''Prince Namor, the Sub-Mariner'' #19 (Nov. 1969). Marvel Comics.</ref>

Stingray aids Namor and the [[Inhumans|Inhuman]] [[Triton (comics)|Triton]] against a group of Atlanteans who destroy an [[ocean liner]] and falsely claim to have done so for Namor. The culprit is revealed to be Atlantean [[warlord]] [[Attuma]], who is eventually defeated.<ref>''Prince Namor, the Sub-Mariner'' #31 (Nov. 1970). Marvel Comics.</ref> Stingray assists Namor in a search for his father Leonard Mackenzie, with Mackenzie being accidentally killed in a battle with villains [[Llyra]] and [[Tiger Shark (Marvel Comics)|Tiger Shark]].<ref>''Prince Namor, the Sub-Mariner'' #39 (July 1971) & #41 \u2013 45 (Sep. 1971 \u2013 Jan. 1972). Marvel Comics.</ref>

After brief appearances in titles the [[Hulk]]<ref>''Hulk'' #221 (Mar. 1978). Marvel Comics.</ref> and [[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]] respectively,<ref>''Defenders'' #62 \u2013 64 (Aug. \u2013 Oct. 1978). Marvel Comics.</ref> Newell becomes a regular guest-star in the title ''[[Marvel Two-In-One]]''. The character moves his oceanographic facilities and research to [[Hydro-Base]], an artificial island previously used by the insane [[ecology|ecologist]] [[Doctor Hydro]] (occupied by Dr. Henry Croft and the passengers of an [[airplane]], who were captured by Hydro and changed into [[Mermaid|merfolk]] known as the [[Hydro-Men]]).<ref>''Marvel Two-In-One'' #64 (June 1980). Marvel Comics.</ref> As Stingray, Newell aids [[Fantastic Four]] member the [[Thing (comics)|Thing]]; Triton and heroine the [[Scarlet Witch]] against the villains the [[Serpent Squad]] and company [[Roxxon Oil]] to prevent them from obtaining the [[Magic item|artifact]] the [[Serpent Crown]].<ref>''Marvel Two-in-One'' #64 \u2013 66 (June \u2013 Aug. 1980). Marvel Comics.</ref> Stingray is present when [[Mister Fantastic]] cures Croft and the other passengers, and aids the Thing, and Inhumans [[Gorgon (Inhuman)|Gorgon]] and [[Karnak (comics)|Karnak]] against [[Maelstrom's Minions]], who steal a dose of the healing compound.<ref>''Marvel Two-in-One'' #71 (Jan. 1981). Marvel Comics.</ref>

Newell marries [[Diane Arliss]], the sister of Todd Arliss (the real name of villain Tiger Shark)<ref>''Marvel Two-in-One'' #74 (Apr. 1981). Marvel Comics.</ref> and leased part of Hydro-Base to superhero team the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]], and becomes an associate member of the team.<ref>''Avengers'' #262 (Dec. 1985). Marvel Comics.</ref> During the first \"[[Armor Wars]]\" storyline, Avenger [[Iron Man]] confronts Stingray, mistakenly believing that Newell's suit was based on technology stolen from [[Stark Industries|Stark Enterprises]], forcing Stark to publicly fire Iron Man to protect his company from being affected by his current actions after he confirmed that Stingray's suit had been independently created.<ref>''Iron Man'' #226 (Jan. 1988). Marvel Comics.</ref> Stingray aids the Avengers when Hydro-Base is invaded by [[Heavy Metal (Marvel Comics)|Heavy Metal]], a team of [[robot]] villains (consisting of the [[Super-Adaptoid]], [[Machine Man]], [[Awesome Android]], [[TESS-One]], and the [[Kree]] [[Sentry (Kree)|Sentry 459]]).<ref>''Avengers'' #289 \u2013 290 (Mar. \u2013 Apr. 1988). Marvel Comics.</ref>

During the \"[[Acts of Vengeance]]\" storyline, the Hydro-Base is damaged by an attack from [[Doctor Doom]]'s [[Doombot]]s and sinks, with Stingray aiding the Avenger [[Quasar (Wendell Vaughn)|Quasar]] in a [[Marine salvage|salvage]] operation,<ref>''Quasar'' #5 (Dec. 1989). Marvel Comics.</ref> and then joining a group of reserve Avengers in a battle against the [[Mad Thinker]]'s Awesome Android.<ref>''Avengers Spotlight'' #27 (Dec. 1989). Marvel Comics.</ref> In the title ''[[Marvel Comics Presents]]'' Stingray encounters now-[[brother-in-law]] Tiger Shark and after a battle work together to save Diane Arliss, who is trapped after a [[cave-in]].<ref>''Marvel Comics Presents'' #53 \u2013 54 (July 1990) & #55 \u2013 56 (Aug. 1990). Marvel Comics.</ref> The character aids the Avengers; [[Canadian]] super team [[Alpha Flight]] and the [[Supreme Soviets|People's Protectorate]] during ''The Crossing Line'' storyline<ref>''Avengers'' #319 \u2013 324 (July \u2013 Oct. 1990). Marvel Comics.</ref> and with Namor battles a [[subterranea (geography)|subterranean]] army.<ref>''Namor'' Annual #1 (Jan. 1991). Marvel Comics.</ref>

After featuring in a ''Marvel Comics Presents'' solo story<ref>''Marvel Comics Presents'' #173 (Feb. 1995). Marvel Comics.</ref> the character appears in the first storyline of the third volume of the title the ''Avengers''<ref>''Avengers'' #1 \u2013 3 (Feb. \u2013 Apr. 1998). Marvel Comics.</ref> and in the title ''[[Mutant X (comics)|Mutant X]]''.<ref>''Mutant X'' Annual #1 (May 1999). Marvel Comics.</ref> Stingray aids the Avengers in an extended storyline against futuristic villain [[Kang the Conqueror]];<ref>''Avengers'' #43 \u2013 45 (Aug. \u2013 Sep. 2001); #46 (Nov. 2001); #52 \u2013 55 (May \u2013 Aug. 2002). Marvel Comics.</ref> appears in the [[Limited series (comics)|limited series]] ''Avengers/Thunderbolts''<ref>''Avengers/Thunderbolts'' #3 (June 2004). Issues #1 \u2013 6 (May \u2013 Sep. 2004). Marvel Comics.</ref> and the final issues of the third volume of the Avengers.<ref>''Avengers'' #501 \u2013 503 (Oct. \u2013 Dec. 2004). Marvel Comics.</ref>

During the \"[[Civil War (comics)|Civil War]]\" storyline, Stingray is a member of the Secret Avengers \u2013 led by [[Captain America]] \u2013 who oppose the [[Registration acts (comics)#2006 Superhuman Registration Act|Superhuman Registration Act]].<ref>''Civil War'' #5 (Nov. 2006). Issues # 1 \u2013 7 (July 2007 \u2013 Jan. 2007). Marvel Comics.</ref> After Captain America's arrest and subsequent death, Stingray accepts [[Iron Man|Tony Stark]]'s (Iron Man's [[alter ego]]) offer of a full pardon, and joins [[Fifty State Initiative|The Initiative]].<ref>''Iron Man/Captain America: Casualties of War'' #1 (Feb. 2007); ''Civil War: Battle Damage Report'' #1 (May 2007); ''Avengers: The Initiative'' #1 (June 2007). Marvel Comics.</ref>

The character appears in another solo story in the second volume of ''Marvel Comics Presents''<ref>''Marvel Comics Presents'' vol. 2, #11 (Sep. 2008)</ref> and in an ongoing basis in the title ''Avengers: The Initiative''.<ref>''Avengers: The Initiative'' #3 (Aug. 2007); #8 (Feb. 2008); #10 (May 2008); #14 \u2013 15 (Aug. \u2013 Sep. 2008); #21 \u2013 23 (Feb. \u2013 Mar. 2009); #23 (June 2009). Marvel Comics.</ref>

During the \"[[AXIS (comics)|AXIS]]\" storyline, Stingray is among the heroes recruited by an inverted Doctor Doom to join his team of Avengers. This group tries to focus on the innocent people put in danger where Scarlet Witch, now uncaring of morality, attacks Latveria. Stingray does his part by rescuing several people from a debris-filled river. His actions earn him the respect of U.S. Agent.<ref>''Avengers World'' #15 (2015). Marvel Comics.</ref>

As part of the \"[[All-New, All-Different Marvel]],\" Stingray is seen as part of [[Deadpool]]'s [[Mercs for Money]].<ref>''Deadpool'' vol. 4 #1. Marvel Comics.</ref> It is later revealed that Stingray is actually a [[double agent]] assigned to spy on Deadpool's team by Captain America.<ref>''Deadpool'' vol. 4 #3. Marvel Comics.</ref>

During the \"[[Secret Empire (comics)|Secret Empire]]\" storyline, Stingray appears as a member of the Underground which is a resistance movement following Hydra's takeover of the United States.<ref>''Secret Empire'' #1 (June 2017). Marvel Comics.</ref>

Stingray and Diane were later on a cruise that was attacked by Tiger Shark. Their fight took place underwater until Namor crashed it demanding their allegiance. When Stingray tried to reason with Namor, he is attacked by the War Sharks summoned by Namor forcing Tiger Shark to side with Namor. This attack left him near-death, but he survived as confirmed when the Avengers confronted Namor and the Defenders of the Deep about this action.<ref>''Avengers'' Vol. 8 #9\u201310. Marvel Comics.</ref>

During the \"Iron Man 2020\" event, Stingray fought Captain Barracuda and his army of Robo-Buccaneers at the [[Bermuda Triangle]]. The battle was crashed by [[Machinesmith]] who persuaded the Robo-Buccaneers to join the A.I. Army. As the Robo-Buccaneers take up the offer and leave with Machinesmith, Stingray states to a confused Captain Barracuda that he will have to knock him out now.<ref>''Iron Man 2020'' Vol. 2 #2. Marvel Comics.</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Walter Newell designed and wears the Stingray battlesuit, an armored exoskeleton suit composed of a superhard artificial cartilage designed mainly for underwater use.<ref name=\":0\">''Marvel Avengers: The Ultimate Character Guide'' #1</ref> The Stingray battlesuit enhances his strength and durability to superhuman levels, which allow him to operate within the crushing pressures of the ocean depths. His suit is equipped with an oxygen-diffusing system providing breathable air almost indefinitely, allowing him to breathe underwater. The suit also gives him enhanced swimming speed and its streamlined wings allow him to glide through air for great distances. The Stingray battlesuit's chief offensive weapon is a powerful electrical discharge device built into the exoskeleton,<ref>{{Cite web |author=C. B. R. Staff |date=2019-01-06 |title=Earth's Mightiest Arsenal: 20 Of The Most Powerful Avengers Weapons, Ranked |url=https://www.cbr.com/avengers-powerful-weapons-ranked/ |access-date=2022-11-19 |website=CBR |language=en-US}}</ref> able to project bolts of up to 20,000 volts through air or water and released through the gloves. The suit grants protection against radiation.<ref>''Deadpool & the Mercs for Money'' Vol 2 #2</ref>

Additionally, Walter Newell has a gifted intellect and has a Ph.D. in oceanography. He is an experienced oceanographer, and a skilled inventor of experimental oceanographic equipment.<ref name=\":0\" />

==Other versions==
===Age of Apocalypse===
In the ''[[Age of Apocalypse]]'' reality, Stingray is the captain of the submarine ''Excalibur'' which transports refugees to Avalon.<ref>''X-Calibre'' #2 (1995). Marvel Comics.</ref>

===Marvel Zombies===
Stingray is seen as part of the zombified horde of heroes in ''Ultimate Fantastic Four''.<ref>''Ultimate Fantastic Four'' #21\u201323 (2005). Marvel Comics.</ref>

===Mutant X===
In the ''[[Mutant X (comics)|Mutant X]]'' reality, Stingray is a member of the [[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]]. Stingray was on a mission in Atlantis when the [[Sentinel (comics)|Sentinels]] attack [[Avengers Mansion]]. He joined up with the Defenders to keep the Goblin Queen from getting into the Nexus of All Realities.<ref>''Mutant X'' #32 (June 2001). Marvel Comics.</ref>

===Secret Wars===
During the \"[[Secret Wars (2015 comic book)|Secret Wars]]\" storyline, a version of Stingray resides in the futuristic [[Battleworld]] domain of Technopolis (which is based on a world in which everyone wears armors to live and survive due to an unknown airborne virus). This version is a spy outfitted with an advanced suit of armor developed by [[Iron Man 2020|Arno Stark]]. He is sent to spy on Kiri Oshiro by Arno Stark and [[Kingpin (comics)|Wilson Fisk]] and ends up trying to kill the girl and her friend Lila Rhodes after stating that he can't leave behind any witnesses. Kiri manages to defeat Stingray before he can kill Lila.<ref>''Armor Wars'' #2. Marvel Comics.</ref>
