'''Supergiant''' is a [[supervillain]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. Created by [[Jonathan Hickman]] and [[Jerome Ope\u00f1a]], Supergiant first appeared in ''[[Infinity (comic book)|Infinity]]'' (October [[2013 in comics|2013]]).

The character has made several appearances in other media such as animated television and video games.

==Publication history==
Supergiant first appeared in ''[[Infinity (comic book)|Infinity]]'' (October [[2013 in comics|2013]]) and was created by [[Jonathan Hickman]] and [[Jerome Ope\u00f1a]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbr.com/exclusive-introducing-the-black-order-of-infinity-part-three/|title=EXCLUSIVE: Introducing the Black Order of \"Infinity\" Part Three|date=10 May 2013}}</ref>

==Fictional character biography==
Supergiant is a member of [[Thanos]]' supervillain team [[Black Order (comics)|Black Order]]. While searching for [[Thanos]]' son [[Thane (comics)|Thane]] during the ''[[Infinity (comic book)|Infinity]]'' storyline, Supergiant and [[Corvus Glaive]] laid siege to the [[X-Mansion|Jean Grey School for Higher Learning]] and defeated the [[X-Men]]. They left after realizing that Thane was not there.<ref>''New Avengers'' vol. 3 #9. Marvel Comics.</ref>

When the Black Order seized [[Wakanda]], Supergiant was left in control of [[Black Bolt]] who she would mentally order to activate the [[Illuminati (comics)|Illuminati]]'s hidden bombs in Wakanda Necropolis.<ref>''Infinity'' #5</ref> When the Illuminati arrived to save Black Bolt, Supergiant used Black Bolt's ability to defeat the heroes. Upon activating the bomb, Supergiant was faced by [[Maximus (comics)|Maximus]] who had the trigger. Maximus triggered the bomb, but also used [[Lockjaw (comics)|Lockjaw]] to transport Supergiant along with the bomb to a distant uninhabited planet where she perished in the explosion.<ref>''Infinity'' #6. Marvel Comics.</ref>

During the \"No Surrender\" arc, Supergiant came back as a psychic projection, along with the rest of the Black Order, thanks to the [[Challenger (comics)|Challenger]] who had set them up against [[Grandmaster (Marvel Comics)|Grandmaster]]'s [[Lethal Legion]].<ref>''Avengers'' #676. Marvel Comics.</ref> During the battle in Antarctica, the Black Order retreat after [[Corvus Glaive]] is killed. Supergiant stays to take control of [[Jane Foster (comics)|Thor]], but is dissipated by Lethal Legion member Ferene the Other.<ref>''The Avengers'' #680. Marvel Comics</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Supergiant has telepathic abilities, which allow her to mind-control anyone and feed on their intellect.<ref>''New Avengers'' Vol 3 #9</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Cheeda |first=Saim |date=2019-05-12 |title=10 Things The MCU Changed About The Black Order |url=https://screenrant.com/mcu-black-order-changes/ |access-date=2022-11-20 |website=ScreenRant |language=en-US}}</ref> At some point, she was transformed into a psionic entity.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Harn |first=Darby |date=2021-08-24 |title=10 Things Only Comic Book Fans Know About Thanos\u2019 Black Order |url=https://screenrant.com/thanos-black-order-marvel-comics-facts-trivia/ |access-date=2022-11-20 |website=ScreenRant |language=en-US}}</ref> Out of her transformation, Supergiant gained the power to phase through living beings and obstacles.<ref name=\":0\">{{Cite web |last=Moroca |first=Alexandra |date=2022-02-12 |title=MCU: Every Member Of Thanos\u2019 Black Order, Ranked By Power |url=https://www.cbr.com/mcu-thanos-black-order-power-ranked/ |access-date=2022-11-20 |website=CBR |language=en-US}}</ref> She also became immune to physical damage.<ref name=\":0\" />

In the episode \"Come and Gut Your Love\", Supergiant works with another ex-girlfriend of Star-Lord's, Lucy, to attack him after discovering he is [[J'son (character)|J'son]]'s son.
