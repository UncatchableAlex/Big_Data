'''Dontrell''' \"'''Cockroach'''\" '''Hamilton''' is the name of a fictional supervillain appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. The character is most closely related to [[Luke Cage]], one of the early black [[Marvel Comics|Marvel]] superheroes.

==Publication history==
The character first appears in ''[[Power Man and Iron Fist|Luke Cage, Power Man]]'' #28 (December 1975), produced by [[Don McGregor]], [[George Tuska]], and [[Vince Colletta|Vinnie Colletta]].

==Fictional character biography==
Growing up in Harlem, Dontrell was petrified by the night's noise. However, over time, he gradually become accustomed to them, besides the [[cockroach]]es that infested his home. These early experiences lead to an affinity for cockroaches and \"Cheese Snipz\".<ref>''Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe 2006 #5</ref>

Later in life, Hamilton turns to a life of crime, becoming a mob enforcer (alias \"Cockroach\") for [[Piranha Jones|Ray \"Piranha\" Jones]].<ref>''Power Man'' #28</ref> During an investigation of Dontrell, [[Luke Cage|Power Man]] is attacked by Dontrell and Ray's men at the Harlem River docks. Power Man is eventually knocked out and carried back to Ray's penthouse, but breaks free and attacks Ray. Power Man and Ray fall into a pool of piranhas, and Dontrell seals them in and escapes.<ref>''Power Man'' #30-31</ref>

Dontrell and Ray escape conviction and move to [[Stamford, Connecticut]]. They demand [[Racketeering|protection]] money from the locals, who refuse, but are eventually accepted into the community. However, three women Vita Buchetta, Ann Repucci, and Annette Cortese shame the members of the community for accepting Dontrell and Ray, while secretly using them to use them to start their own criminal careers. When [[Terror Inc.]] is hired by an unknown party to kill Ray, Dontrell cannot defend him because his custom shotgun, \"Josh\", keeps jamming. Upon examining the information from the cartridges, Terror Inc. realized that Ray had hired them. Terror Inc. saved Ray from the mobsters and the arriving [[Punisher]] as Dontrell chased after Terror Inc. in their cars as Terror Inc. killed off Ray.<ref>''Terror Inc.'' #6-7</ref>

During the ''[[Shadowland (comics)|Shadowland]]'' storyline, Dontrell appears as a member of Nightshade's Flashmob and attacks [[Power Man (Victor Alvarez)|Victor Alvarez]]. Dontrell fired on Victor until his gun was compressed by Luke Cage upon his arrival with Iron Fist. The Flashmob was later taken, arrested and taken to Ryker's Island, but Nightshade's solicitor [[Big Ben Donovan]] secured Dontrell's release.<ref>''Shadowland: Power Man'' #2</ref>

Hamilton later appears as one criminal, discussing the reward that [[Tombstone (comics)|Tombstone]] had offered to anyone who could recover the Supersoul Stone from Luke Cage and Iron Fist.<ref> {{Cite comic|writer = David Walker|penciller = Sanford Greene|inker = Sanford Greene|colorist = Lee Loughridge|letterer = VC's Clayton Cowles|editor = Jake Thomas|title = Power Man and Iron Fist|volume = 3|issue = #2|date = 16 March 2016|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}} </ref> When a group of \"[[Pre-crime|preemptive]]\" vigilantes assault inactive villains, Hamilton joins the relatives of several of the victims in requesting help from the Heroes for Hire.<ref> {{Cite comic|writer = David Walker|penciller = Flaviano|inker = Flaviano|colorist = John Rauch|letterer = VC's Clayton Cowles|editor = Jake Thomas|title = Power Man and Iron Fist|volume = 3|issue = #6|date = 13 July 2016|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}} </ref> Cage has Hamilton assist with investigating the \"Preemptive Strike\" by having him consult with criminals like Piranha Jones.<ref> {{Cite comic|writer = David Walker|penciller = Sanford Greene and Flaviano|inker = Sanford Greene and Flaviano|colorist = John Rauch|letterer = VC's Clayton Cowles|editor = Jake Thomas|title = Power Man and Iron Fist|volume = 3|issue = #7|date = 17 August 2016|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}} </ref> After discovering that the Preemptive Strike work for [[Alex Wilder]], Hamilton betrays the Heroes for Hire and joins Alex Wilder's [[Pride (comics)|New Pride]] alongside [[Black Mariah (comics)|Black Mariah]], Cottonmouth, and [[Gamecock (comics)|Gamecock]].<ref> {{Cite comic|writer = David F. ==Powers and abilities==
Dontrell Hamilton has no superpowers but is a skilled marksman, using advanced pieces of artillery. His first weapon was a six-barreled shotgun built by himself and named \"Josh\". 

==Other versions==

===Earth-X===
In the ''[[Earth-X]]'' reality, Dontrell \"Cockroach\" Hamilton's history is the same, but in the present he has died.<ref>''Universe X Special'' #1</ref>

===House of M===
In the ''[[House of M]]'' reality, Dontrell \"Cockroach\" Hamilton is a mutant gangster with a cockroach appearance.<ref>''House of M: Avengers'' #1</ref>

==In other media==
* Cockroach Hamilton appears in ''[[The Avengers: Earth's Mightiest Heroes]]'' episode \"To Steal an Ant-Man\" as a member of [[Crossfire (comics)|William Cross]]' gang.
* Cockroach Hamilton appears in the [[Luke Cage (season 2)|second season]] of ''[[Luke Cage (TV series)|Luke Cage]]'', portrayed by [[Dorian Missick]]. This version is a petty criminal who runs an off-the-books casino and was recently released from prison with help from corrupt NYPD Detective [[Rafael Scarfe]].<ref>{{cite episode|title=Soul Brother #1|series=Marvel's Luke Cage|credits=Liu, Lucy (director); Cheo Hodari Coker (writer)|network=[[Netflix]]|airdate=June 22, 2018|season=2|number=1}}</ref> Additionally, Hamilton is physically abusive towards his girlfriend Andrea \"Drea\" Powell and her son CJ. After running afoul of [[Luke Cage (Marvel Cinematic Universe)|Luke Cage]], the latter nearly kills him until one of Hamilton's neighbors intervenes.<ref>{{cite episode|title=Straighten It Out|series=Marvel's Luke Cage|credits=Green, Steph (director); Akela Cooper (writer)|network=[[Netflix]]|airdate=June 22, 2018|season=2|number=2}}</ref> Hamilton subsequently sues Cage for his injuries and hires [[Big Ben Donovan|Benjamin Donovan]] to represent him in court. However, Hamilton is later killed by [[Bushmaster (Marvel Comics)|Bushmaster]].<ref>{{cite episode|title=All Souled Out|series=Marvel's Luke Cage|credits=Lemmons, Kasi (director); Ian Stokes (writer)|network=[[Netflix]]|airdate=June 22, 2018|season=2|number=5}}</ref>
